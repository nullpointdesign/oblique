The Vision · A Visão

Concept Statement

Porto, Oblique is a photographic exploration of Porto, Portugal, seen through a lens that resists the obvious. Rather than monuments or postcard vistas, this body of work captures the city’s layered, lateral, and surprising realities: the lived textures, fleeting gestures, and quiet juxtapositions that define Porto as it is, not as it is advertised. The oblique gaze invites viewers to encounter the city’s spirit in the overlooked, the ambiguous, and the in-between.

About · Sobre

Photographer Bio Angle

Catherine Anderson is a photographer drawn to the subtle and the sidelong. With a practice rooted in documentary and fine art photography, her work foregrounds the overlooked and the ambiguous, seeking meaning in the margins. In Porto, Oblique, Catherine brings an outsider's sensitivity and a resident's patience, having spent extended time in Porto during 2023–2024, immersing in its rhythms beyond the tourist gaze.

Audience · Público

Market Positioning

Porto, Oblique is positioned for readers and collectors of contemporary photography, urban studies, and travel culture who seek authenticity over spectacle. It appeals to those interested in the real, lived experience of cities—fans of street photography, design-conscious book buyers, and travelers who value depth over checklist tourism. The book’s minimal, design-forward presentation makes it ideal for the coffee table, appealing to both art lovers and the culturally curious.

Context · Contexto

Comparable Titles

  • Saul Leiter: Early Color — Steidl
  • Raymond Depardon: Glasgow — Editions du Seuil
  • Alex Webb: The Suffering of Light — Thames & Hudson
  • Vivian Maier: Street Photographer — PowerHouse Books

Porto, Oblique stands apart by focusing on Porto’s unique visual language, offering a contemporary, non-touristic perspective rarely seen in English-language photobooks.

Structure · Estrutura

Proposed Book Structure

  • Foreword — Contextual essay on the oblique gaze and Porto’s visual culture
  • Photo Sections (4–6 thematic chapters):
    • Thresholds — Entrances, windows, liminal spaces
    • Surfaces — Walls, textures, layers of time
    • Transit — Movement, crossings, fleeting encounters
    • Assemblages — Found compositions, urban still lifes
    • Afterlight — Dusk, reflections, ambiguity
  • Short texts — Interspersed reflections or quotes (from local writers, poets, or the photographer)
  • Image Index — Captions, locations, dates
  • About the Photographer
